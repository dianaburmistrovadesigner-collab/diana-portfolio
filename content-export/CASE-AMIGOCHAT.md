## Overview

### The Challenge

Independent content creators, freelancers, and small business owners are trapped between simple-but-limited AI tools and powerful-but-chaotic ones. They juggle multiple subscriptions, lose context switching between platforms, and waste time on tool management instead of creative work.

### The Vision

AmigoChat is a unified AI workspace that brings the best AI models together in one friendly interface — with customizable AI personalities, project-based workspaces, and a personal AI coordinator that eliminates decision fatigue.

## Research — Understanding the Problem

### 2.1 Competitive Analysis — 31 Competitors

Analyzed 31 competitors across three categories. Key finding: market leaders like ChatGPT have a critical weakness — closed ecosystems. This creates an opportunity for an open, unified alternative.

| Category | Examples | Key Insight |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Direct (AI Aggregators) | Poe, TypingMind, Merlin | Good multi-model access, but poor UX and no workspace organization |
| Indirect (Single-Model AI) | ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini | Powerful but locked ecosystems, no cross-model flexibility |
| Substitutes (Productivity) | Notion AI, Zapier, Canva | Solve specific tasks but don't provide unified AI experience |

### 2.2 User Survey — 33 Respondents

Distributed a survey to validate hypotheses. 32 completed responses. 34% qualified as MVA (Minimum Viable Audience) — professionals who actively use and pay for AI tools.

| Стат | Инсайт |
|------|--------|
| **66%** | suffer from AI forgetting context |
| **56%** | experience inconsistent AI results |
| 69% | want a better solution |
| 53% | use only free tools (cost sensitivity) |
| 56% | spend 5–60+ min/day switching tools |
| 34% | are MVA (professionals who pay for AI) |

*Подпись к иллюстрации:* The daily reality of juggling multiple AI tools — and why MVA users are desperate for a unified solution

## Define — Who Are We Designing For?

### 3.1 Personas

Two key personas representing 100% of the target audience. They reflect the core market tension: the choice between simplicity and power.

#### Maya Chen — 40% of audience

- **Archetype:** "The Overwhelmed Beginner"
- **Role:** Small business owner, new to AI
- **Needs:** Simplicity · Guidance · Quick wins
- **Frustrations:** Overwhelmed by choice · Complexity · Fear of making mistakes
- **Quote:** "Why is this so complicated? There must be an easier way."

#### Alex Rivera — 60% of audience

- **Archetype:** "The Frustrated Power User"
- **Role:** Freelancer, experienced AI user
- **Needs:** Power · Organization · Transparency
- **Frustrations:** No client organization · Loss of context · Lack of control

## Ideate — Framing the Opportunity

### 4.1 Problem Statement

> "Independent content creators feel trapped between simple-but-limited AI tools and powerful-but-chaotic ones. This forces them to sacrifice either capabilities or peace of mind."

### 4.2 How Might We...

- How might we eliminate the trade-off between simplicity and power?
- How might we organize the chaos for power users without adding complexity for beginners?
- How might we create an emotional connection with an AI tool?
- How might we make users feel in control without overwhelming them with options?

### 4.3 User Scenario — Maya's First Success Story

Maya needs to create a social media post for her bakery. Instead of spending 3 hours juggling ChatGPT for text, Midjourney for images, and Canva for layout — she opens AmigoChat. The AI coordinator asks what she needs, suggests the right assistant, and she creates a complete post in **20 minutes**. She enters a state of creative flow.

### Iteration 1 · Shipped MVP / В проде

Represented in the Figma key scenario (150+ screens across desktop, mobile web, and native iOS adaptations):

Prioritized in research (Kano Model) and journey maps but did not ship before AmigoChat was acquired by Overchat in early 2025. Not cut because they lacked value — the product roadmap ended with the acquisition.

- Project Workspaces (Must-Be for Alex — multi-client organization)
- Personal AI Coordinator (Delighter for Maya — guided tool selection)

- Templates library

*You'll see these in personas, empathy maps, and Kano tables above — they informed the MVP direction even where dedicated screens weren't built.*

## Design — Information Architecture & Visual Language

### 6.1 Card Sorting Results

Card sorting with users informed the navigation structure, revealing 3 natural levels:

- **Global Navigation** — Workspace switching (for Alex's multi-client needs)
- **Primary Navigation** — Chats, Templates, AI Brain, Settings
- **Contextual Navigation** — Model selection, assistant picker within chat

### 6.2 Sitemap

- **Public pages:** Landing, Pricing, About
- **Authentication:** Sign up, Log in (Google/Apple)
- **App:** Workspaces → Projects → Chats, AI Store, AI Brain, Settings (Profile, Subscription, Appearance, Language, Notifications)
